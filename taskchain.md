## Success criteria

- One canonical charter repository and package/display direction are approved.
- Useful history from both Alistaire repositories is preserved with attribution and rollback.
- The portfolio contract and authority matrix is reviewed against every repository-local charter and accepted with conflicts recorded.
- A neutral non-operational contract steward is assigned.
- Canonical bytes, identities, digests, clocks, replay domains, reason codes, and extension rules pass cross-language fixtures.
- Repository `1` or successor is independently chartered with issuer/revoker, key-custody, recovery, and incident boundaries.
- Portfolio incident, freeze, correction, cache invalidation, claim withdrawal, and rollback command are assigned.
- Every repository has a declared narrow responsibility, inputs, outputs, authority boundary, failure posture, and rollback obligation.
- Every record family has a semantic owner, independent identity, accepted mappings, correction/revocation rules, and unsupported-state behavior.
- Repository `0`/`1` portable-trust contracts cover device identity, observation, proposal, capability, execution, receipt, reconciliation, loss, replacement, and recovery.
- QSO, evidence, review, payment, engineering, and publication paths have accepted pairwise and triple-overlap witnesses.
- A documentation-only release can be reviewed without implying runtime, device, security, payment, or deployment capability.

## Non-goals

- Implementing or deploying an unrestricted autonomous agent.
- Claiming consciousness, sentience, AGI, human-equivalent cognition, universal compromise detection, or guaranteed device security.
- Inspecting or controlling devices without ownership or explicit permission.
- Traffic interception, exploitation, retaliation, counter-intrusion, or surveillance.
- Silent self-modification, credential discovery, remote writes, remediation, deployment, publication, or payments.
- Allowing prompts, ceremonial language, issue text, signatures, interfaces, successful commands, or dependencies to grant authority.
- Treating the matrix, a dependency edge, passing fixture, transport success, rendered view, or publication as operational acceptance.
- Reimplementing subsystem responsibilities inside the charter repository.
- Training on private or external data without provenance, license, consent, and policy controls.
- Shipping a production application before contracts, fixtures, security review, incident command, and rollback evidence exist.
