## Rollback criteria

Withdraw or rebind the candidate if:

- competing identities remain without an accepted compatibility rule;
- migration loses history, attribution, license, privacy, or rollback;
- a component or document defines the contract granting its own authority;
- repository roles, record identities, capability status, or contributor stop conditions collapse;
- a currentness record points to the wrong head or suppresses a sibling lineage;
- canonical bytes, namespaces, identities, clocks, replay, correction, or revocation remain ambiguous;
- kernel, runtime, projection, aggregate, and disposition records cannot be distinguished;
- documentation, fixtures, passing CI, feature inclusion, or contributor routing is treated as an accepted live binding;
- projection or aggregation inflates evidence or authority;
- controlled lifecycle routes diverge;
- independent implementations disagree;
- migration broadens scope, certainty, precision, or authority or lacks a restored-state witness;
- private data, credentials, keys, or recovery material enter public artifacts;
- `UNKNOWN` becomes success, evidence is duplicated, validation is not reproducible, or required approvals are absent;
- freeze, invalidation, recovery, claim withdrawal, or rollback cannot execute independently.

Restore the last reviewed documentation state, preserve failed-candidate evidence, invalidate derived claims, and do not begin runtime or device work from an unapproved charter.
