## Rollback criteria

Withdraw or rebind the candidate if:

- competing identities or package claims remain without an accepted compatibility rule;
- migration loses history, attribution, license, privacy, or rollback;
- a component defines the contract granting its own authority;
- repository roles or record identities collapse;
- canonical bytes, semantic namespaces, identities, clocks, replay, correction, or revocation remain ambiguous;
- kernel, runtime, projection, aggregate, and disposition records cannot be distinguished;
- an inventory treats declaration, documentation, fixtures, registry references, internal records, or passing CI as accepted live binding;
- projection or aggregation inflates evidence or authority;
- independent implementations disagree;
- a migration broadens scope, certainty, precision, or authority or lacks a restored-state witness;
- private data, credentials, keys, or recovery material enter public artifacts;
- `UNKNOWN` becomes success, evidence is duplicated, controlled planning routes diverge, or validation is not reproducible;
- freeze, invalidation, recovery, claim withdrawal, or rollback cannot execute independently;
- claims exceed evidence or required approvals are absent.

Restore the last reviewed documentation state, preserve failed-candidate evidence, invalidate derived claims, and do not begin runtime or device work from an unapproved charter.
